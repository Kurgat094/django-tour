Discover the Majesty of Mount Kenya

Mount Kenya, Africa's second-highest peak, offers breathtaking landscapes, diverse wildlife, and thrilling adventures. From its snow-capped peaks to lush forests and alpine meadows, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a haven for nature lovers and adventurers alike. Explore scenic hiking trails, encounter unique flora and fauna, and immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of the Kikuyu people who revere the mountain as sacred.

How to Get to Mount Kenya?

Mount Kenya is protected by Mount Kenya National Park and is located approximately 200 kilometers (125 miles) northeast of Nairobi. To reach the mountain, you can fly to Kenya through Nairobi using Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO). From Nairobi, the hiking point of Mount Kenya can be accessed via road transport using either public or private vehicles.

How Hard is it to Climb Mount Kenya?

Climbing Mount Kenya is quite challenging regardless of the peak you are climbing. A high level of fitness is essential to navigate steep sections and long distances. Ascending Mount Kenya is generally more difficult than descending due to the altitude. Climbers suffering from altitude sickness should have an experienced guide to help manage the situation.

Climbing Routes

Mount Kenya has several climbing routes, each offering a unique experience. The most popular routes include:

  • Sirimon Route: A scenic route that offers stunning views and is suitable for both beginners and experienced climbers.
  • Naromoru Route: The fastest route to Point Lenana, but it can be crowded.
  • Chogoria Route: A less crowded route with beautiful landscapes and wildlife sightings.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mount Kenya is during the dry seasons, which are from January to March and June to October. These months offer clear skies and stable weather conditions, making it ideal for hiking and climbing.
